Baklava: The Soul of the Mediterranean

Description

Baklava! You may be asking yourself, "What does that even mean?" I honestly do not know either, but gosh! Does it taste amazing! Baklava is a pastry made of many layers of phyllo dough and is usually filled with a variety of nuts. Most commonly sweetened with syrup, yet it is also common to use honey and top it with pistachios.

Ingredients

  • 1 16 oz package of phyllo dough
  • 1 lb of chopped nuts
  • 1 cup of butter
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinammon
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of ground sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup of honey

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F. While waiting, butter the bottom and sides of a 9x13 inch pan.
  2. Chop nuts and toss with cinammon and set aside. Unwrap phyllo dough and cover it with a damp cloth to keep it moist. Place a sheet of phyllo over the pan and butter thoroughly. Repeat this 8 times. Sprinkle the nut mixture on top. Reapeat a similar process, but only 4 phyllo sheets this time. You top layer must end with 6 to 8 sheets deep.
  3. Once done, sprinkle the remains of your nut mixture on top and using the knife, cut into diamond shapes. Place in the oven for 50 minutes.
  4. While waiting for baklava, boil sugar and water until the sugar has melted. Let it simmer for 20 minutes or so.
  5. Remove the baklava from the oven and pour the syrup evenly throughout the whole surface. Let it cool down and get ready to let your palate melt.
